F A C T S
Field Team
The playing field is a rectangular area with dimensions and zones as shown above. Each team will put a maximum of seven players on the field during each point.
The playing field should be essentially flat, free of obstructions and afford reasonable player safety. The minimum is a count of five players on the field during each point.
The perimeter lines surround the playing field and consist of two sidelines along the length and two endlines along the width. Each team must designate a captain and a spirit captain to represent the team.
The perimeter lines are not part of the playing field. A team may make unlimited substitutions after a goal is scored and before their team signals readiness for the pull.
The goal lines are the lines that separate the central zone from the end zones and are part of the central zone.
The immediate surroundings of the playing field shall be kept clear of movable objects.
If play is obstructed by non-players or objects within three metres of the perimeter line, any obstructed player or thrower in possession may call “Violation”.
